# SPM 2023 — Question 15: Index Numbers
**Paper:** 3472/2 | **Topic:** Index Numbers | **Total marks:** 10

## Full Question Transcription

**15** Jadual 4 menunjukkan maklumat tentang empat bahan yang digunakan untuk membuat sejenis kek.
*Table 4 shows information about four ingredients used to make a type of cake.*

| Bahan / *Ingredient* | Harga (RM) per kg 2021 / *Price (RM) per kg 2021* | Harga (RM) per kg 2023 / *Price (RM) per kg 2023* | Indeks harga 2023 dengan 2021 sebagai tahun asas / *Price index 2023 with 2021 as base year* |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| A | 25.00 | 27.50 | *x* |
| B | 16.00 | 18.00 | 112.5 |
| C | *y* | 22.50 | 150 |
| D | 7.50 | 9.00 | 120 |

*Jadual 4 / Table 4*

**(a)** Cari nilai *x* dan nilai *y*.
*Find the value of x and of y.* **[3 marks]**

**(b)** Indeks gubahan untuk kos membuat kek itu pada tahun 2023 dengan 2021 sebagai tahun asas ialah 127.5. Nisbah bagi bahan-bahan A, B, C dan D yang digunakan ialah 3 : 2 : *m* : 4.
Cari nilai *m*.
*The composite index for the cost of making the cake in the year 2023 with 2021 as the base year is 127.5. The ratio of the ingredients A, B, C and D used is 3 : 2 : m : 4.
Find the value of m.* **[2 marks]**

**(c)** Dijangkakan bahawa harga kek itu akan meningkat sebanyak 20% dari tahun 2023 ke tahun 2025.
*It is expected that the price of the cake will increase by 20% from the year 2023 to the year 2025.*

**(i)** Cari indeks gubahan untuk kos membuat kek itu pada tahun 2025 dengan 2021 sebagai tahun asas.
*Find the composite index for the cost of making the cake in the year 2025 with 2021 as the base year.* **[1 mark]**

**(ii)** Nyatakan harga kek itu pada tahun 2025, jika harganya pada tahun 2021 ialah RM100 per kg.
*State the price of the cake in the year 2025, if the price in the year 2021 is RM100 per kg.* **[1 mark]**

**(iii)** Cari peratus kenaikan harga bahan C dari tahun 2021 ke tahun 2025 jika harga bahan lain tidak berubah dari tahun 2023 ke tahun 2025.
*Find the percentage increase of ingredient C from the year 2021 to the year 2025 if the price of other ingredients remains unchanged from the year 2023 to the year 2025.* **[3 marks]**

## K/P Mark Identification (from marking scheme)

> Mark allocation: (a) 3 marks | (b) 2 marks | (c)(i) 1 mark | (c)(ii) 1 mark | (c)(iii) 3 marks = **10 total**

### Part (a) — 3 marks

| Mark | Type | Step | Working shown |
|------|------|------|---------------|
| 1 | **K1** | Apply price index formula forward to find *x* | *x* = (27.50 / 25.00) × 100 = **110** |
| 2 | **K1** | Rearrange price index formula to set up equation for *y* | (22.50 / *y*) × 100 = 150 → 22.50/*y* = 1.5 |
| 3 | **P1** | Solve for *y* | *y* = **RM15** (i.e. RM15.00 per kg) |

### Part (b) — 2 marks

Using weightages 3, 2, *m*, 4 with indices 110, 112.5, 150, 120 respectively, denominator = 9 + *m*:

| Mark | Type | Step | Working shown |
|------|------|------|---------------|
| 1 | **K1** | Set up composite index equation with *m* as unknown | [3(110) + 2(112.5) + *m*(150) + 4(120)] / (3+2+*m*+4) = 127.5 → (1035 + 150*m*) / (9+*m*) = 127.5 |
| 2 | **P1** | Cross-multiply, simplify, solve for *m* | 1035 + 150*m* = 1147.5 + 127.5*m* → 22.5*m* = 112.5 → ***m* = 5** |

### Part (c)(i) — 1 mark

| Mark | Type | Step | Working shown |
|------|------|------|---------------|
| 1 | **K1** | Chain-link composite indices: 20% increase → Ī(2025/2023) = 120 | Ī(2025/2021) = 127.5 × (120/100) = **153** |

### Part (c)(ii) — 1 mark

When base year price = RM100, the price in any target year numerically equals the composite index (since P_target = index/100 × 100 = index). No working needed — "state" question.

| Mark | Type | Step | Working shown |
|------|------|------|---------------|
| 1 | **P1** | State price in 2025 directly from chain index | P_2025 = **RM153** |

### Part (c)(iii) — 3 marks

Other ingredients (A, B, D) unchanged from 2023 to 2025 → their indices based on 2021 remain at their 2023/2021 values: I_A = 110, I_B = 112.5, I_D = 120.
Total weightage (with *m* = 5): 3 + 2 + 5 + 4 = **14**.

| Mark | Type | Step | Working shown |
|------|------|------|---------------|
| 1 | **K1** | Set up composite equation for 2025/2021 with I_C as the only unknown | [3(110) + 2(112.5) + 5(I_C) + 4(120)] / 14 = 153 → (1035 + 5I_C) / 14 = 153 → I_C = **221.4** |
| 2 | **K1** | Use I_C to find price of C in 2025 from its 2021 base price | (P_2025 / 15) × 100 = 221.4 → P_2025 = **RM33.21** |
| 3 | **P1** | Calculate percentage increase of C from 2021 to 2025 | % increase = (33.21 − 15) / 15 × 100% = **121.4%** |

> **Shortcut note:** % increase = I_C − 100 = 221.4 − 100 = 121.4% (directly from the index, no need to find P_2025 first). Both methods earn full marks.

### Template: IDX-11 — Find Unknown Integer in Weightage Ratio from Composite Index

**Harvestable marks:** 2 out of 2 (K1, P1)
**Subpart:** (b) typically
**SPM format:** All individual indices known; weightages given as a ratio with one unknown integer *m*;
composite index given; find the value of *m*

#### What it tests
- K1: Substitute all 4 known indices and the algebraic ratio (with *m*) into the composite formula; cross-multiply
- P1: Solve the resulting linear equation for *m*

#### Structure

**Fixed:**
```
"The composite index for the cost of making [product] in the year [current] with [base] as the base year is [Ī].
The ratio of the ingredients A, B, C and D used is [a] : [b] : m : [d]."
"Find the value of m."
Answer format: m = [positive integer]
```

**Variable:**
```
- The composite index Ī (given explicitly)
- The 4 individual price indices (all known — from part a or the table)
- The ratio values a, b, d (known integers); m is the only unknown
- Which position in the ratio m occupies (not always third)
```

#### Variable Slots
```json
{
  "composite_index": { "type": "float", "range": [110.0, 145.0], "decimal_places": 1 },
  "individual_indices": {
    "count": 4,
    "type": "float",
    "range": [100.0, 175.0],
    "rule": "all known before part (b) — either given in table or computed in part (a)"
  },
  "known_ratio_values": {
    "a": { "type": "integer", "range": [1, 6] },
    "b": { "type": "integer", "range": [1, 6] },
    "d": { "type": "integer", "range": [1, 6] }
  },
  "m_answer": {
    "type": "positive_integer",
    "range": [1, 10],
    "rule": "must be a clean positive integer — fractional m is not SPM-realistic"
  }
}
```

#### Constraints
```
1. Solving for m must yield a clean positive integer (no decimal or negative result)
2. After substituting known values, the equation must reduce to: (A + B·m) / (C + m) = Ī
   where A, B, C are known numbers — this is always the form when m appears in exactly one term
   of both numerator and denominator
3. The coefficient of m in the numerator (index of item with unknown ratio) must differ from Ī
   (otherwise the equation is degenerate — m cancels)
4. Composite index must sit in a plausible food-cost range [110, 145]
5. All individual indices must be from the table or computed in part (a) — do not introduce new unknowns
```

#### Solution Steps
```
Step 1 | K1
What student writes: Substitute all 4 indices and their ratio values into the composite formula:
  [a·I_A + b·I_B + m·I_C + d·I_D] / (a + b + m + d) = Ī
  → simplify numerator: (known_sum + I_C·m) / (known_denom + m) = Ī
Pattern cue: "write every term — four on top, four in the denominator. Keep m as a letter."
Concept note: "the ratio values ARE the weightages — treat them as numbers directly"

Step 2 | P1
What student writes: cross-multiply → known_sum + I_C·m = Ī·(known_denom + m)
  → collect m: (I_C − Ī)·m = Ī·known_denom − known_sum
  → m = (Ī·known_denom − known_sum) / (I_C − Ī)
Pattern cue: "after cross-multiplying, move all m terms to one side — then divide to isolate m"
Concept note: "I_C is the index of the ingredient whose ratio is unknown — it should differ from Ī,
otherwise check your setup"
```

#### Hint Ladder
```
Tier 1: "Write the composite index formula. Put ratio values as weightages. Treat m as a letter.
Set the whole thing equal to [Ī]."

Tier 2: "Numerator: [a]×[I_A] + [b]×[I_B] + m×[I_C] + [d]×[I_D] = [known_sum] + [I_C]m
Denominator: [a] + [b] + m + [d] = [known_denom] + m
Set up: ([known_sum] + [I_C]m) / ([known_denom] + m) = [Ī]. Cross-multiply."

Tier 3: "[known_sum] + [I_C]m = [Ī] × ([known_denom] + m)
         [known_sum] + [I_C]m = [Ī·known_denom] + [Ī]m
         ([I_C] − [Ī])m = [Ī·known_denom − known_sum]
         m = ___ / ___ = ___"

Tier 4 (full static worked example — SPM 2023 numbers):
"Indices: A=110, B=112.5, C=150, D=120. Ratio: 3:2:m:4. Ī=127.5.
[3(110) + 2(112.5) + m(150) + 4(120)] / (3+2+m+4) = 127.5
(330 + 225 + 150m + 480) / (9+m) = 127.5
(1035 + 150m) / (9+m) = 127.5
1035 + 150m = 1147.5 + 127.5m
22.5m = 112.5 → m = 5"
```

#### Common Wrongs
```
Error 1: Uses number of items (4) as denominator instead of (a+b+m+d) → targets Step 1
Error 2: Forgets to include m in the denominator (treats denominator as a+b+d only) → targets Step 1
Error 3: Cross-multiplies but only distributes Ī to (known_denom), not to the m term
          → gets Ī·known_denom + m instead of Ī·known_denom + Ī·m → targets Step 2
Error 4: Collects m terms but subtracts wrong direction: writes (Ī − I_C)m instead of (I_C − Ī)m
          → gets a negative m → targets Step 2
Error 5: Does not cross-multiply — tries to "guess and check" integer values of m without algebra
          → often finds correct answer but gets no method mark
```

#### How IDX-11 differs from other weightage templates

| Template | Structure | Result |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| IDX-04 | All 4 weightages as algebraic expressions in *m* and *n* | Ratio m:n (then ingredient ratio) |
| IDX-07 | 2 known weightages + 2 independent unknowns *p*, *q* | Expression: p = A − Bq |
| **IDX-11** | 3 known ratio values + 1 unknown integer *m* | Single integer: m = [value] |

### Template: IDX-12 — Find Unknown Ingredient Index When Other Prices Frozen; Calculate % Increase

**Harvestable marks:** 3 out of 3 (K1, K1, P1)
**Subpart:** (c)(iii) typically
**SPM format:** Composite index known for a future year (via chain-link from earlier subpart);
"other ingredients unchanged from [intermediate year] to [target year]" → their indices based on
original base year are frozen; one ingredient's index is unknown; solve, then find its % price increase

#### What it tests
- K1: Recognise that "price unchanged from year X to Y" means the ingredient's index based on the
      original base year = same as its index at year X; set up composite equation for target year
      with one unknown index
- K1: Solve for the unknown index; use it with the ingredient's base year price to find target year price
- P1: Calculate % increase (from base year to target year) using the price or the index directly

#### Structure

**Fixed:**
```
"Find the percentage increase of ingredient [C] from the year [base] to the year [target]
if the price of other ingredients remains unchanged from the year [intermediate] to the year [target]."
Answer format: percentage increase = [1dp]%
```

**Variable:**
```
- Which ingredient is the "unknown" one (whose price changes from intermediate to target)
- The target year composite index (chain-linked from earlier subpart)
- The individual indices of all other ingredients based on the base year (frozen at intermediate year values)
- The base year price of the unknown ingredient (from the table)
- Weightages for all ingredients (from part b result)
```

#### Variable Slots
```json
{
  "target_composite_index": {
    "type": "float",
    "range": [120.0, 180.0],
    "decimal_places": 2,
    "note": "carried from the chain-link subpart"
  },
  "frozen_ingredient_indices": {
    "count": 3,
    "note": "unchanged from intermediate year — use same indices as computed for intermediate year"
  },
  "unknown_ingredient_base_price": {
    "type": "float",
    "range": [5.00, 30.00],
    "decimal_places": 2
  },
  "total_weightage": {
    "type": "integer",
    "range": [8, 20],
    "note": "sum of all weightages including the unknown ingredient's — from part b"
  }
}
```

#### Constraints
```
1. Solving for the unknown index I_unknown must yield a clean 1dp result (e.g. 221.4, not 221.37...)
2. Target price of unknown ingredient = (I_unknown / 100) × base_price must round to exactly 2dp
3. % increase = I_unknown − 100 must give a clean 1dp result (always satisfied if I_unknown is 1dp)
4. The unknown ingredient's I_unknown must be substantially > 100 (it's changing while others are frozen —
   the whole point is that it drives the overall composite up)
5. Weightages used must come from the earlier part of the same question — do not re-derive
6. "Other ingredients unchanged" means their 2023/2021 indices carry forward unchanged to 2025/2021 —
   make this explicit in hints
```

#### Solution Steps
```
Step 1 | K1
What student writes:
  Recognise: "unchanged from 2023 to 2025" → I_A, I_B, I_D based on 2021 stay at their 2023/2021 values
  Set up: [w_A·I_A + w_B·I_B + w_C·I_C + w_D·I_D] / Σw = chain_composite_index
  with I_C as the only unknown
Pattern cue: "if a price doesn't change from 2023 to 2025, its index relative to 2021 also doesn't change —
              plug the 2023/2021 index directly into the 2025 equation"
Concept note: "price index = (P_current/P_base)×100. If P_current doesn't move, index doesn't move."

Step 2 | K1
What student writes: Solve for I_C; then find P_2025(C) = (I_C / 100) × base_price_C
Pattern cue: "once you have the index, finding the price is just (index/100) × base price"
Concept note: "you can skip finding the price: % increase = I_C − 100 directly from the index"

Step 3 | P1
What student writes: % increase = (P_2025 − P_base) / P_base × 100%
                   OR equivalently: % increase = I_C − 100
Pattern cue: "price index of 221.4 means the price is 221.4% of its original → increase = 121.4%"
Concept note: "always check: % increase must be positive (price went up) and match the direction"
```

#### Hint Ladder
```
Tier 1: "If the price of ingredient A doesn't change from 2023 to 2025, what happens to
its price index (based on 2021)? It stays the same. Write in those frozen index values.
Only ingredient C's index is unknown."

Tier 2: "Composite 2025/2021 = [chain_index]. Weightages: A=[w_A], B=[w_B], C=[w_C], D=[w_D].
[[w_A]×[I_A] + [w_B]×[I_B] + [w_C]×I_C + [w_D]×[I_D]] / [Σw] = [chain_index].
Solve for I_C."

Tier 3: "([known_sum] + [w_C]·I_C) / [Σw] = [chain_index]
[known_sum] + [w_C]·I_C = [chain_index × Σw]
I_C = ([chain_index × Σw] − [known_sum]) / [w_C] = ___
% increase = I_C − 100 = ___%"

Tier 4 (full static worked example — SPM 2023 numbers):
"Chain composite 2025/2021 = 153. Frozen: I_A=110, I_B=112.5, I_D=120. Weightages: 3,2,5,4. Σw=14.
[3(110) + 2(112.5) + 5·I_C + 4(120)] / 14 = 153
(1035 + 5I_C) / 14 = 153
5I_C = 2142 − 1035 = 1107 → I_C = 221.4
P_2025(C) = (221.4/100) × 15 = RM33.21
% increase = (33.21 − 15) / 15 × 100% = 121.4%
(Or: % increase = 221.4 − 100 = 121.4% directly)"
```

#### Common Wrongs
```
Error 1: Uses the NEW composite index (153) as each frozen ingredient's index, instead of their
          2023/2021 values — misunderstands "unchanged price" as "unchanged composite"
          → targets Step 1
Error 2: Sets denominator to the number of items (4) instead of total weightage (14) → targets Step 1
Error 3: Uses the chain-link index (153) directly to find C's price, skipping the solve-for-I_C step
          → gets P = (153/100) × 15 = RM22.95, which is wrong → targets Step 1
Error 4: Correctly finds I_C = 221.4 but calculates % increase as 221.4% instead of 221.4 − 100 = 121.4%
          → confuses index with % increase → targets Step 3
Error 5: Finds P_2025(C) = RM33.21 but divides by 33.21 instead of 15 when computing % increase
          → gets (33.21−15)/33.21 × 100 = 54.8% → targets Step 3
Error 6: Uses the wrong base year price for C (e.g., uses 22.50 instead of 15) → targets Step 2
```

### Variant Note for (c)(ii) — Base Price = RM100: Price Equals Index Numerically

When a question says: *"State the price of [product] in [target year], if the price in [base year] is RM100"*

> **Pattern:** P_target = (chain_index / 100) × 100 = chain_index
> The price in the target year numerically equals the composite/chain index value.

This is a 1-mark "state" question requiring no working. The answer is read directly from the previously computed chain index.

**Common wrong:** Student computes (127.5/100) × 100 = 127.5 (uses composite index before chain-linking instead of the chain-linked value 153). Always use the chain index for the target year, not the intermediate year index.
